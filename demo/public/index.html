The Messy Middle of Creating

I used to think great writers had it all figured out. That they sat down each morning, opened their laptop, and words just flowed onto the page like water from a tap. That somewhere between their brain and their fingertips was a direct pipeline to brilliance.

I was wrong.

The truth is messier, more human, and ultimately more encouraging. Every writer‚Äîfrom the Pulitzer Prize winners to the person crafting their first newsletter‚Äîgoes through the same uncertain dance of creation.

This morning, I stared at a blank page for twenty-seven minutes. I opened three different browser tabs, checked my email twice, and considered making another cup of coffee even though my mug was still half full. My mind felt like scattered papers on a desk‚Äîlots of ideas, no clear order.

But here's what I've learned: the mess IS the process.

Those twenty-seven minutes weren't wasted time. They were the necessary pause before the breakthrough. The mental throat-clearing before the real conversation begins. The slow warm-up that every engine needs before it can run smoothly.

When I finally started typing, the words didn't come perfectly formed. The first sentence was clunky. The second didn't connect to the first. But I kept going, trusting that momentum would build meaning.

And it did.
